We Are Platforma: Born from Crisis, Built for Impact

The idea for the Doshka Foundation emerged in the spring of 2023. After months of volunteering in Kyiv—purchasing and delivering medical supplies to those in need—Alexis, the founder of Doshka Foundation, realized that the way humanitarian aid was being provided was fundamentally flawed.

While many efforts were driven by enthusiastic individuals, the sourcing, procurement, storage, shipping, and distribution of supplies were erratic. There was a lack of management, process, coordination, planning, and, above all, basic tools.

💡

The Turning Point

"The turning point came when he discovered that some colleagues were buying supplies for the front lines at local pharmacies, rather than sourcing them directly from medical distributors in Kyiv. A simple phone call could have secured larger quantities at better prices."

The Human Cost of Broken Systems

The current fragmented approach creates devastating inefficiencies that hurt everyone in the humanitarian ecosystem.

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦

For Aid Recipients

  • Significant delays in receiving critical supplies due to coordination failures
  • Duplicate deliveries to some while others receive nothing
  • No visibility into what aid is available or when it might arrive
🏢

For Humanitarian Workers

  • Excessive time spent on logistics coordination instead of frontline work
  • Burnout and frustration from preventable inefficiencies
  • Impossible reporting requirements to multiple donors with different standards
💝

For Donors

  • Trust erosion due to lack of transparency
  • No reliable way to track where money actually goes
  • Impact uncertainty with no reliable outcome measurement
